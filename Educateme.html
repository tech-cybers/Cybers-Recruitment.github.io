Phishing Scam Alert!

Phishing tricks victims into handing over credentials for all kinds of confidential accounts, such as email,
corporate intranets, and more

SIMPLE STEPS TO IDENTIFY AND PROTECT YOURSELF FROM PHISHING

  1. Inspect the URL for grammar and spelling mistakes: To trick users into thinking they are on an official site, the fraudsters will stick as closely as they can to the
    real address and make small changes to the spelling.
  2. An offer that seems too good to be true: This is to compell you to click on the link which may be attached with malwares and
    viruses that may get installed in your device.
  3. Watch out for URL redirects, where you're subtly sent to a different website with identical design
  4. Change password regularly, don’t use the same password again and again for different accounts.
  5. Genuine brands won’t ask for your sensitive information. Always investigate before clicking the link from the brand or professionals.

IMPACT OF A SUCCESSFUL PHISHING

Being a victim of phishing scams can have serious consequences like

  1. The theft of personal data (which criminals can sell to third parties
  2. The possible theft of money when taking over our bank details.
  3. Losing control of your computer and having to pay a ransom to recover it
  4. All the computers connected to the internal network infected by some type of virus.
  5. Identity theft.
